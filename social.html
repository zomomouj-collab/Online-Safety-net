Understanding Social Media Risks

Privacy and Personal Information

Social media platforms often require users to share personal information. This can include your name, location, and even contact details. It's essential to understand how this information can be used or misused by others.

Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying occurs when someone uses digital platforms to harass or intimidate others. It can lead to severe emotional distress and should be taken seriously. Encourage friends to speak up against any form of bullying they may experience online.

Digital Footprint

Your digital footprint is the trail of data you leave behind when using the internet. This can include social media posts, comments, and even likes. Be mindful of the content you share, as it can impact your reputation long-term.

Real-Life Scenarios

Consider scenarios where sharing too much online can lead to real-world consequences. For instance, oversharing vacation plans can leave your home vulnerable to burglary.

Privacy Settings Checklist

Regularly review your privacy settings to ensure that only the right people can see your content. Here’s a checklist to help:

  • Set profiles to private.
  • Limit who can send friend requests.
  • Regularly audit app permissions.

Smart Social Media Habits

Adopt safe practices while using social media:

  • Think before you post.
  • Always verify sources of information before sharing.
  • Be cautious of unknown friend requests.

Cyberbullying Resources
