Understanding Scams

Types of Scams

  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity.
  • Pyramid Schemes: Business model that recruits members via a promise of payments for enrolling others.
  • Lottery Scams: False notifications claiming the recipient has won a lottery or prize.
  • Online Shopping Scams: Fake websites or listings selling non-existent products.

Real-Life Scenarios

Consider the case of an individual receiving an email claiming to be from their bank, asking for confirmation of account details. This is a phishing attempt.

A victim of a lottery scam might receive a call informing them they’ve won a cash prize but must pay fees to claim it. This is a common tactic used by scammers.

Warning Signs

  • Unsolicited communications requesting personal information.
  • Too-good-to-be-true offers.
  • Pressure tactics urging immediate action.

Protection Practices

Always verify the authenticity of requests by contacting the organization directly. Use two-factor authentication on sensitive accounts and be cautious about sharing personal information.

Interactive Checklists

Before engaging with unknown entities, ask yourself:

  • Do I know this sender?
  • Am I being pressured to act quickly?
  • Is the offer realistic?

Helpline Resources

If you believe you have been a victim of a scam, contact your local authorities or helpline such as the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) at 1-877-FTC-HELP (1-877-382-4357).
